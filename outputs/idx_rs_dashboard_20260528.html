WHAT THIS DASHBOARD DOES

Surfaces momentum leaders on the Indonesian Stock Exchange using Relative Strength analysis. Methodology follows Minervini / Qullamaggie principles: RS percentile ranking, multi-timeframe momentum screening, sector rotation analysis, and Stage 2 uptrend confirmation.

RS score measures each stock's performance relative to IHSG (^JKSE). A stock with RS score 150 has outperformed IHSG by 50% over the composite 12-month window. Percentile ranks that score within the liquid universe — a stock at percentile 95 is outperforming 95% of all liquid IDX stocks.

RS is a screening tool, not an entry trigger. Use it to build a watchlist, then confirm price structure (Stage 2 base, VCP, volume contraction) before entry.

RS SCORE FORMULA

RS = (0.4 × perf_63d + 0.2 × perf_126d + 0.2 × perf_189d + 0.2 × perf_252d) / same_for_IHSG × 100

Score of 100 = equal to IHSG. Above 100 = outperforming. Scores of 300–600 are common for strong leaders in trending markets.

LIQUIDITY FILTER

Only stocks with 30-day average volume ≥ 1,000,000 are included. The universe count in the header reflects this filtered set.

LOADING A NEW CSV

Click ⬆ LOAD CSV in the top right to upload a fresh idx_rs_rankings_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS.csv from main.py. The dashboard fully rebuilds client-side — all metrics, sector composites, and cross-TF calculations recompute in the browser. No server required.

TABS & FILTERS
RS / 1M / 3M LEADERS — TOP% PILLS

TOP% pills (ALL / 1% / 2% / 5% / 10% / 20%) cut the list to the top N stocks computed against the full liquid universe. TOP 2% always means the same absolute count regardless of other active filters — it's a true cross-sectional top-N, not a relative filter.

CROSS-TF LEADERS — MIN TF COUNT

Shows stocks simultaneously strong across multiple timeframes. MIN TF COUNT sets the minimum number of timeframes (1M/3M/6M/12M) where the stock must be in the top 10% simultaneously.

tf=4: Dominant on all timeframes — rarest, highest conviction
tf=3, shape > 0: Strong and still accelerating — primary watchlist
tf=3, shape < 0: Former leader decelerating — caution, may be topping
tf=2, shape > 0, avg > 85: Emerging leader — watch for graduation to tf=3
MOMENTUM GAINERS

All 4 conditions must be true: pct_1m > pct_3m, pct_3m > pct_12m, pct_1m ≥ 60, pct_1m − pct_3m ≥ 10. These are stocks whose RS is building right now.

SECTORS

Composite score combining multi-timeframe breadth (1M/3M/6M), ceiling (top-5 strength), and average. Genuine rotation = broad breadth across all three timeframes. A spike in 1M breadth alone with weak 3M/6M = concentrated move, not sector-wide rotation.

DAILY WORKFLOW (QULLAMAGGIE METHOD)
STEP 1 — MARKET CONTEXT

Check IHSG vs its key moving averages before using RS data aggressively. One day above SMA20 after a multi-month decline is not sufficient for active entries. In weak market conditions, use the dashboard to identify stocks that held up during the drawdown — build the watchlist, wait for market confirmation.

STEP 2 — SECTOR ROTATION (SECTORS TAB)

Identify top 2–3 sectors by composite score. Prioritise candidates from these sectors. Genuine rotation = broad breadth across 1M/3M/6M simultaneously. A spike in 1M breadth alone with weak 3M/6M is a concentrated move, not sector-wide rotation.

STEP 3 — PERSISTENT LEADERS (CROSS-TF TAB)

MIN TF COUNT 3 or 4 → Accelerating Only → SMA50 + SMA200. This gives you the highest-conviction, currently-active leaders with Stage 2 confirmation and positive momentum direction.

STEP 4 — FRESH MOMENTUM (MOMENTUM TAB)

RS Δ Rising + Near 52W Hi + SMA200. Stocks whose RS is building right now, at or near highs, in a confirmed uptrend. Highest accel scores with positive Δ MOM are the most urgent watchlist additions.

STEP 5 — CROSS-REFERENCE

Stocks appearing across multiple tabs simultaneously — Cross-TF leaders AND Momentum gainers AND in a top-3 sector — are the highest-conviction setups. When they appear, prioritise them for chart review.

STEP 6 — CHART REVIEW (REQUIRED)

The dashboard is a quantitative screen. Chart-level validation is always required before entry:

Price obeys rising 10-day and 20-day MAs (tight, controlled action)
Base quality: VCP or flat base with volume drying up during the consolidation
Entry trigger: breakout from base on volume expansion (1.5–2× average)
Avoid: wide/loose price action, high volume on down days, broken SMA50
